This Isn’t a Résumé Problem

Virtue signals, false fixes, and the quiet exploitation of unemployed workers in a broken system.

Let's name what's happening.

The job market, for many people right now, is brutal. Not challenging. Not “in transition.” Brutal.

Layoffs are happening not because people underperformed, but because companies are chasing cost savings and AI-enabled efficiencies with a kind of frothy, almost religious fervor. Those on the other side of the pink slip are left holding the bag, wondering what they did wrong when the real answer is: nothing. They were simply no longer part of the equation.

The Exploitation of Desperation

When people are at their lowest — uncertain, anxious, and sometimes ashamed — there's an entire cottage industry that pops up with a promise: We can fix it.

This chart visualizes the gap between the advertised impact of many paid career services and their more modest, realistic outcomes in a difficult market.

AI, Egoism, and the Illusion of Control

Meanwhile, companies are marching toward automation with almost zero public accountability for what that means. AI-driven systems are already gatekeeping access to jobs — and doing it behind layers of obfuscation, bias, and unexplainable logic.

That's not advice. That's shame dressed up as strategy. And in many cases, it's egoism disguised as altruism.

What Can Be Done?

Protect your cash.

Start with powerful free tools: AI chatbots, résumé generators, and YouTube tutorials.

Build your story.

A good résumé or profile won't fix a broken market, but it can help you articulate your value more clearly.

Question motives.

Do they care about your outcome, or their funnel metrics?

Consider creating something.

If you're stuck, don't wait for the market to find you. Build something small. Write. Start a community.

If you're in the thick of this, know this: you're not alone. And you're not broken.
